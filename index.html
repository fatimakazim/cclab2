Leave Me Alone!

The Unknown Creature: "SN-1"

Reproduction

SN-1 reproduces through fragmentation, breaking into two or more fragments that develop into new individuals. Though it can potentially reproduce, biologists state that the reproduction of SN-1 only happens in rare cases with unknown reasons or causes.

Adaptations

SN-1 can survive in a wide range of marine water bodies. It can live through areas with ocean surface temperatures ranging from 31 degrees Celsius to -2 degrees Celsius and from the ocean surface to a depth of 2,500 meters!

Interesting Fact!

We know that SN-1 doesn't like attention, and it hates when things get close to it. It changes its pupil color to black to avoid attention at night or when the lights are closed. That way, SN-1 makes itself less visible in the dark.

Name and Appearance

The name "SN-1" comes from where it is found and the order in which the unknown creatures are found. "S" means sea creature; "N" means north; and "1" means that it is the first unknown creature that the team found in the area. SN-1 has a black ball-shaped body, an eye at the center, and a pupil that changes color depending on the situation. The pupil is blue most of the time.

Where it is Found

SSN-1 was found near the Great Pacific Garbage Patch in the North Pacific Ocean. The ocean water in that area is highly polluted with toxic chemical disposal from factories and energy-generating constructions. The team found the creature floating alone close to the water's surface and tried to search for similar species; however, they found none. SN-1 is currently placed in one of the specially-made glass tanks in a secret laboratory under the observation of a group of biologists.

Behavior and Reactions

SN-1 spent most of its time slowly floating and looking around its surroundings. It is very cautious of almost any moving object that is bigger than itself. Once something appears in its sight, its eye will lock on the subject until the subject disappears. Getting too close to it might turn on its stress response, where it starts to shake, open its eye wider, and its pupil turns red. The stress response remains for a short period, opening its eyes wide for a few seconds after the subject leaves. However, the pupil immediately changes to its initial color(blue) once the subject moves away.

What does it eat

It feeds directly on micro-bacteria that can also tolerate the toxic pollutants in the ocean water. Often, those bacteria flow in the toxic water along with the gas formed through chemical reactions and decomposition in the water. Surprisingly, if you place the creature into normal ocean water, it can also consume micro-bacteria that are commonly seen there! In other words, it feeds on almost every type of bacteria that live in salt water.
